History of Gaming Developing

Turning point 1990

If by the mid-1980s national industries were formed, by the 1990s they had overcome this insularity, absorbing more backward and less hasty neighbors. Two world centers of the gaming industry have been formed - Japan with all Asian region (China, Korea, Taiwan) and the United States (Euro-Atlantic region). And at this stage it was cultural differences that hindered the way the industry further globalization and brought it to a common standard. But in Europe, this standard, as well as the largest market and production, was called "USA" and in the east "Japan" and then "China."

nintendo entertainment system(NES)

But even inside the U.S. there were different processes. In the late 1980s, large studios like Lucas Arts, not wanting to play by the rules of Nintendo, went to the PC. And while the consoles were fully satisfied with the 16-bit, the PC began to widely use the VGA video adapter, and then SVGA: showing 256 colors instead of 16 - thus very much raising the quality of the graphics. The inertia of consoles, in this respect, provided the advantage of the PC in the 1990s. They were extremely difficult to adapt to consoles with gamepads without sticks.

After the release of PlayStation One and other members of the 5th generation in the second half of the 1990s, 3D began a victorious procession across all genres and platforms. With the scale of games began to grow and budgets, and studios required more and more qualified staff, which increased the salary and attractiveness of video games for many talented people. But failure now meant huge financial losses. Inevitably, the dependence of large and not very corporations on banks, which at first only issued loans, but soon became investors, who set their conditions for maximum profit. At the same time came out and the main problem of the PC - various components, operating systems (up to the 2000s many sat under DOS) and software.

Playstation One

When it became clear that Sega was threatening Nintendo's position, the latter creatively approached the issue of competition. There was talkouts about violence in games. In 1993, the American press began to pay attention to adult content in several games. Nintendo clung to this, deciding through the U.S. Senate to lobby for the creation of a common rating system, blocking the air of Sega with their super-profitable series like Mortal Kombat. Representatives of Sega openly stated that the conflict was artificially inflated by competitors. Creating an unnecessarily rigid system would close the path for Sega to whole genres that were not too interested in the Big N. But the hype gave the opposite desired result - the circulation of "nightmare" games on this wave were completely sold out, and in 1994 there was a famous ESRB.
