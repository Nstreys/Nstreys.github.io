History of Gaming Developing

Europe 1980 and The rise of the "pirate"

Europe lagged behind the U.S. in the field of interactive entertainment. In 1978, the Interton VC-4000 (similar to the Atari VCS 2600) was released in Germany. But these fakes could not oppose anything to the American consoles. In the PC market, the sane alternative to the American Apple II, Commodore PET or TRS-80 was not even seen. At the same time, the games were not taken seriously and advertised only as one of the features of the PC. Actually, that's why the console giants were in no hurry to occupy the market on the other side of the Atlantic, especially when its not yet mastered and is on the verge of collapse.And in 1982 in the UK, and further everywhere, came the famous ZX Spectrum, bypassed the sales of even the Commodore 64, and became the best-selling PC in the world. At the same time began a really massive, albeit artisanal, production of games. English developers went into complete surrealism, releasing platformers about toilets-killers. The intensity of the absurdity was such that the press seriously discussed drug addiction among developers similar to the spread of drugs among British musicians. The energy of the British market has become a powerful growth factor for the video game industries of Spain and Australia, which focused on the English market. Spain's main export was Army Moves, inspired by Rambo, and heist games like Goody, Australians mostly made text quests based on "The Hobbit" by J. R.R. Tolkien. In 1982 alone, Spectrum released 226 games made in the UK. In 1983 this number rose to 1,188, and the number of companies producing them from 95 to 458.

The French gaming industry differed from the British primarily by trying to speak on serious topics, bringing interactive entertainment on the degree of emotional impact on a par with films and books. But, in general, her industry has developed very slowly. Popular then magazine Tilt in 1984 wrote that the French games tried to create something close to the French reality and bearing the imprint of the personalities of their creators.

Over Germany since the time of the Navy was dominated by strict censorship, so that foreign games were constantly blacklisted, and their developers tried not to take risks and acted in the genre of adaptation of board games, and in the future simulators of everything and everything. And for several years they were comfortable alone with themselves. So far, the Netherlands has not planted a large pig for the entire industry, becoming a European center of piracy. There were no laws regulating intellectual property in games and only kind words and prayers could only affect pirates. That they, who perceived it not only as earnings, but also the challenge of their own skills and creativity, did not bother much.


ZX spectrum

And if no one pays for the goods, then no one will produce it. Moreover, the increase in piracy came just at the period of increasing budgets and production cycle of each game. In 1984 alone, 474 studios closed in the UK, and by 1989 there were only 101. In other countries it was even worse. There is either every little thing left, or large corporations from Europe like Ubisoft, which are gaining strength. Pirates have cleared the way for Japanese and American companies like EA, not to mention the giants of Sega, Nintendo and Sony in the mid-1990s, and five years later Microsoft. Very quickly the gaming industry became an international phenomenon, and all large corporations worked all over the world.
