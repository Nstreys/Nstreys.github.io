History of Gaming Developing

Nintendo after situation with Atari

And while the Americans were sitting at the broken trough, Nintendo in 1983 released its legendary Nintendo Entertainment System or simply NES. Taking advantage of all the developments on both sides of the Pacific, it very quickly captured more than 90% of the market, securing a monopoly for the next 10 years.

The release of the NES marked the new rules of the game. Before NES, developers were free to develop games for any console, and all they needed was to buy special equipment. The NES had a special 10NES authorization chip. If the console chip could not detect the chip in the cartridge, the game did not start. Developers needed permission from Nintendo to develop each game. Developers were also forbidden to work for other companies. And games on other consoles were allowed to be released no earlier than 2 years after the release on NES.

nintendo entertainment system(NES)

Nintendo began to produce a hundred games a year, while other companies could count on only a dozen. When Nintendo took over 90% of the market, fixed prices were set. And even though the cost of production was falling, prices did not think to fall. In general, all the charms of any monopoly. The start of sales of NES/Famicon in Japan was so significant that japanese director Osamu Katayama compared it to the opening of Disneyland in Tokyo (it opened at the same time as the launch of NES). This proved to be the most important factor influencing the development of culture in Japan and society as a whole. Such incredible success of NES, first in Japan and then in the rest of the world, has led to several original business solutions.
